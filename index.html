How I Work

Step 01
Start with the data, then talk to users

Quantitative analysis surfaces where the problem is. Qualitative research explains why. I start every initiative by finding the biggest gaps in the funnel, then running exit surveys and user interviews to understand root causes — not guessing at them.

Step 02
Write the strategy before writing requirements

A PRD without a strategy document is a list of features without a point of view. I define the strategic bets, the "why now," and the competitive context before writing a single user story. This keeps the team aligned on what we're building and why it matters.

Step 03
Design for what could go wrong

Every feature has a failure mode. Guardrail metrics, ethical constraints, and explicit risk tables aren't bureaucracy — they're what separates PMs who ship good products from PMs who ship fast. I name the risks and design around them.

Step 04
Tie everything back to revenue

Product decisions aren't made in a vacuum. I build impact models with conservative, base, and optimistic revenue scenarios so stakeholders can make informed prioritization decisions. Engagement metrics are proxies — GMV is the point.
