# User Personas
## AI-Powered Collectibles Discovery Engine

---

## Persona 1: The Focused Collector

**Name:** Marcus, 42  
**Location:** Chicago, IL  
**Occupation:** Financial Advisor  
**Income:** $175K/year  
**Platform Tenure:** 4+ years, 200+ bids placed

### Background
Marcus has collected vintage mechanical watches for 15 years. He knows exactly what he wants — specific references, specific eras, specific conditions. He's a high-intent buyer who will spend $3K–$15K on the right piece without hesitation, but has zero patience for irrelevant results.

### Goals
- Find highly specific items quickly (e.g., "Rolex 1675 GMT Master, matte dial, 1971–1974")
- Stay on top of new listings before they get bid up
- Discover adjacent categories he doesn't know yet (pocket watches, vintage dive gear)

### Frustrations
- "I search for a specific reference and get 50 unrelated watches"
- "I miss listings because the platform doesn't alert me intelligently"
- "The same junk shows up on my homepage every time I log in"

### Behaviors
- Visits platform 4–5x per week
- Uses watchlist heavily
- Reads item descriptions thoroughly
- Compares across multiple listings before bidding

### How the Discovery Engine Helps
- Intent-aware search parses "matte dial 1675 GMT pre-1975 under $12k" correctly
- "Ending soon in watches" urgency module keeps him from missing auctions
- "More Like This" on adjacent items (Heuer chronographs) expands his collection

## Persona 2: The Casual Browser

**Name:** Priya, 31  
**Location:** Austin, TX  
**Occupation:** UX Designer  
**Income:** $95K/year  
**Platform Tenure:** 8 months, 12 bids placed

### Background
Priya started collecting vintage movie posters after decorating her apartment. She's passionate but still learning the market. She has a modest budget ($200–$800 per item) and is easily overwhelmed by choice. She discovers through browsing, not searching.

### Goals
- Find cool, affordable posters she hasn't seen before
- Understand what makes certain items more valuable
- Feel confident she's getting fair prices

### Frustrations
- "The homepage feels random — nothing relates to what I've been looking at"
- "I don't know how to search for what I want when I don't know what it's called"
- "I wish I could see items similar to one I just bid on"

### Behaviors
- Sessions driven by browsing, not search
- Saves items to watchlist but often forgets them
- High mobile usage (65% of visits on phone)
- Responsive to "ending soon" and "trending" signals

### How the Discovery Engine Helps
- Personalized homepage surfaces more posters after her activity
- "Because you watched [item]" shelf creates serendipitous discovery
- Smart price trend filter helps her spot stable vs. rising markets
- Category quiz helps seed recommendations early in her journey

## Persona 3: The Gift Buyer

**Name:** Tom, 55  
**Location:** Boston, MA  
**Occupation:** High School Principal  
**Income:** $120K/year  
**Platform Tenure:** Occasional (2–3 visits/year)

### Background
Tom's son is an avid sports card collector. Tom visits the platform a few times a year to find a gift — usually around birthdays and holidays. He has low domain knowledge and high anxiety about "getting it right." He's willing to spend $300–$1,000 on the right item.

### Goals
- Find something his son will love without knowing the hobby deeply
- Not get ripped off on price
- Complete the purchase quickly and confidently

### Frustrations
- "I don't know what I'm looking for — I just know he likes Mickey Mantle"
- "There's no guidance for someone like me who doesn't know this world"
- "I'm scared I'll buy something fake or overpay"

### Behaviors
- Low session frequency
- High bounce rate from complex pages
- Relies on social proof ("most popular", "bestseller")
- Needs authentication signals prominently displayed

### How the Discovery Engine Helps
- Natural language search: "Mickey Mantle card gift around $500" returns curated results
- Certification/grade filter visible prominently for trust
- "Trending gifts in sports cards" module guides discovery
- Gift-wrap and buyer protection prominently surfaced
